What We're Building

The Goal

The 30-Day IG Challenge runs as a recurring cohort product twice a month — on the 1st and the 15th. The goal of this build is to wire up the full system so that from the moment someone buys, everything that needs to happen just happens: they get course access, land in the roster, receive a confirmation email, get grouped into a pod with 6–9 other people, and receive their Telegram invite — without you touching anything.

How It Works

A single SamCart purchase fires a Zapier zap that grants Kajabi access, logs the buyer to a Google Sheet, and tags them in Flodesk. On cutoff dates, buyers get grouped into pods automatically. Cowork then creates their Telegram group, schedules 5 check-in messages, and sends their welcome email — all from a saved task brief you trigger once.

Once the system is built, your only recurring task is a 10-minute check before each cohort opens to verify everything looks right. After that, the 30-day challenge runs itself — two cohorts a month, zero ongoing manual work per buyer.

To Do
Build the Zap Zapier
What this zap does Fires instantly on every purchase. Three things happen in sequence: Kajabi grants access, buyer lands in the roster, Flodesk sends the confirmation email.
In Zapier, create a new Zap with trigger: SamCart → New Order (select the 30-Day IG Challenge product)
Add Action 1: Kajabi → Grant Offer to Member — map buyer's email from SamCart trigger
Add Action 2: Google Sheets → Create Spreadsheet Row in Master Roster — map: Timestamp, First Name, Email, Purchase Date, set Status = "Pending"
Add Action 3: Flodesk → Add/Update Subscriber — map buyer's email + first name, apply tag: challenge-purchased
Test + Activate Zapier
Run a test purchase and confirm all 3 actions fire correctly — check Kajabi for access, Google Sheet for the new row, Flodesk for the tag

Decision Required
Pod Grouping Logic — Manual or Apps Script
To Do
Make the Decision Google Sheets
Context Zapier can't do the "group into pods of 6–9" math on its own. Someone or something has to fill in the Cohort Month + Pod Number columns before Zap 2 fires. Option A is manual. Option B automates it with a Google Apps Script — ~1 hour of dev work, worth it at expected volume (40–80 buyers/cohort).
Decide: Manual (Option A) or Apps Script (Option B) — consider your expected volume before choosing
Option A — Manual Manual
Document the manual process: before each cutoff date, open the Master Roster, sort by Purchase Date, fill in Cohort Month + Pod Number for each pending buyer in groups of 6–9, then change status to "Assigned"
Add a reminder to your calendar on the 12th and 28th of each month to do pod assignments before cutoff
Option B — Apps Script (Recommended) Google Sheets
Write (or have Claude write) a Google Apps Script that: finds all rows where Status = "Pending," groups them in batches of 6–9, fills in Cohort Month + Pod Number, and sets Status = "Assigned"
Set the script to trigger automatically on the 29th and 13th of each month, OR trigger it manually before each cutoff
Test the script on a sheet with dummy data rows before using it on real buyers
