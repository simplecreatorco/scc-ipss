Every hook is a fill-in-the-blank template designed to work for any niche, any product, any audience. Here's all you need to know before you start.

The Brackets
[identity]
The specific person you're talking to. Not a category — a person. The more specific, the better it works. "Moms of toddlers who haven't had a meal that didn't come from a drive-through in three weeks" beats "busy moms" every time.
[desired outcome]
What they want. Be specific. "Feel better" is vague. "Cook a week's worth of dinners in 90 minutes on Sunday" is a hook.
[struggle / pain point]
What's hard for them right now. Use their words, not yours.
[your product]
Whatever you sell: a guide, planner, workbook, template, course, pattern, printable — anything.
[result]
The proof. Can be financial ($X from one product) or transformational (X people completed the challenge, X five-star reviews, X students got the outcome).
[relatable constraint]
What makes them feel like it won't work for them — limited time, no budget, no experience, life in the way.
One rule above all: Never be clever when you can be clear. Your hook has one job — make the right person stop and feel seen.
